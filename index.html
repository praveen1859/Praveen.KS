About Me

I am currently pursuing an M.Tech in Communication Systems at the National Institute of Technology, Tiruchirappalli, with a strong academic foundation in Electronics and Communication Engineering from NIT Puducherry.

My research interests focus on advanced wireless communication technologies, particularly Orthogonal Time Frequency Space (OTFS) modulation, Free Space Optical (FSO) communication systems, and next-generation Wi-Fi standards (IEEE 802.11be / Wi-Fi 7). I am passionate about exploring innovative approaches to improve reliability, spectral efficiency, and performance in modern communication networks.

I have hands-on experience with MATLAB and Python for simulation-based analysis and performance evaluation of communication systems. I am also familiar with PC assembly and operating systems, and I enjoy solving technical problems by bridging theoretical concepts with practical implementations while continuously learning new technologies.

Featured Work

Projects

Performance Analysis of Hybrid Detection in Optical OTFS over FSO Channels

Jul 2025 - Dec 2025

Studied the performance of DC-biased optical OTFS modulation over FSO channels using hybrid ZF–MMSE detection. Evaluated BER performance across multiple modulation schemes under varying SNR conditions. Simulation results confirmed enhanced error resilience and reliability compared to standalone ZF and MMSE detectors.

Performance Analysis of IEEE 802.11be (EHT) WLAN (Wi-Fi 7)

Jul 2023 - Apr 2024

Designed and developed an analytical model to evaluate saturation throughput in IEEE 802.11be (Extremely High Throughput) WLAN systems. Optimized model parameters to achieve higher throughput and lower latency under varying network loads. Analysed the impact of advanced features such as multi-link operation and NOMA on network performance.
